Chapter 07

Stability Analysis

BIBO stability, Routh-Hurwitz criterion, and relative stability

Chapter Overview

Stability is the most fundamental requirement for any control system. This chapter formalizes BIBO and asymptotic stability, introduces the algebraic Routh-Hurwitz criterion, and connects frequency-domain margins to robustness.

◈ 7.1 — Stability Definitions

BIBO Stability

A system is Bounded-Input Bounded-Output (BIBO) stable if every bounded input produces a bounded output:

$$|r(t)| \leq M_r < \infty \implies |y(t)| \leq M_y < \infty \quad \forall\, t \geq 0$$
Stability Classification
Asymptotically StableAll CL poles in open LHP: $\mathrm{Re}(p_i) < 0$
Marginally StableAt least one simple pole on $j\omega$ axis, none in RHP
UnstableAny pole in open RHP, or repeated poles on $j\omega$ axis

◈ 7.2 — Routh-Hurwitz Criterion

Routh-Hurwitz Theorem

For the characteristic polynomial $P(s) = a_0 s^n + a_1 s^{n-1} + \cdots + a_n$:

The number of RHP roots equals the number of sign changes in the first column of the Routh array.

Constructing the Routh Array

For $P(s)=a_0 s^4 + a_1 s^3 + a_2 s^2 + a_3 s + a_4$:

$$\begin{array}{c|cc} s^4 & a_0 & a_2 & a_4 \\ s^3 & a_1 & a_3 & 0 \\ s^2 & b_1 & b_2 & \\ s^1 & c_1 & & \\ s^0 & d_1 & & \end{array}$$

where $b_1 = \frac{a_1 a_2 - a_0 a_3}{a_1}$, $b_2 = \frac{a_1 a_4}{a_1}=a_4$, $c_1 = \frac{b_1 a_3 - a_1 b_2}{b_1}$, etc.

Special Cases in Routh Array ▸

Case 1: Zero in first column. Replace the zero with a small positive number $\epsilon$, continue, then take $\epsilon \to 0^+$.

Case 2: Entire row of zeros. Form an auxiliary polynomial from the row above, differentiate it, and replace the zero row with the derivative's coefficients.

Worked Example: $s^4+2s^3+3s^2+4s+5$ ▸

Routh Array:

$s^4$: 1, 3, 5

$s^3$: 2, 4, 0

$s^2$: (2·3−1·4)/2 = 1, (2·5−1·0)/2 = 5

$s^1$: (1·4−2·5)/1 = −6

$s^0$: 5

First column: 1, 2, 1, −6, 5. Two sign changes (1→−6, −6→5), so 2 RHP roots: system is unstable.

◈ 7.3 — Relative Stability

A system may be stable but poorly damped. We quantify how far the system is from instability using several measures:

Relative Stability Measures
Gain MarginHow much K can increase before instability
Phase Margin$\phi_m$: additional phase lag at gain crossover before instability
Damping Ratio$\zeta$ of dominant closed-loop poles
Sensitivity Peak$M_s = \max|S(j\omega)|$ where $S=(1+GH)^{-1}$

The relationship between phase margin and damping ratio (second-order approximation):

Phase Margin ↔ Damping $$\phi_m \approx \arctan\!\left(\frac{2\zeta}{\sqrt{\sqrt{1+4\zeta^4}-2\zeta^2}}\right) \approx 100\zeta \;\text{(degrees, for } \zeta<0.7\text{)}$$
