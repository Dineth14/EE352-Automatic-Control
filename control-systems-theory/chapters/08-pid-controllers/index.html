PID Controllers

Proportional, Integral, Derivative Control

1. The PID Algorithm

The Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controller is the most commonly used control algorithm in industry. It calculates an error value $e(t)$ as the difference between a desired setpoint and a measured process variable.

$$ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int_0^t e(\tau) d\tau + K_d \frac{de(t)}{dt} $$ (8.1)

Taking the Laplace transform gives the Transfer Function of the PID controller:

$$ C(s) = K_p + \frac{K_i}{s} + K_d s = \frac{K_d s^2 + K_p s + K_i}{s} $$ (8.2)

2. Ziegler-Nichols Tuning Rules

Ziegler-Nichols proposed heuristic methods for tuning a PID controller without needing the exact plant model. The Ultimate Gain method involves increasing $K_p$ until the system exhibits sustained oscillations (the ultimate gain $K_u$), and recording the ultimate period $T_u$.
