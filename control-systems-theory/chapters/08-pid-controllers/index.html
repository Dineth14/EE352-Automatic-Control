Chapter 08

PID Controllers

Proportional, Integral, Derivative — the most widely used controller

Chapter Overview

The PID controller is the workhorse of industrial control, used in over 90% of all control loops. This chapter covers its structure, each term's effect, tuning methods, and practical design considerations.

◈ 8.1 — PID Transfer Function

Ideal PID Controller $$G_c(s) = K_p + \frac{K_i}{s} + K_d s = K_p\!\left(1 + \frac{1}{T_i s} + T_d s\right)$$

where $T_i = K_p/K_i$ and $T_d = K_d/K_p$.

Effect of Each Term

P, I, D Actions
P (Proportional)Reduces error proportionally. Increases speed but cannot eliminate SSE. Higher $K_p$ → less SSE but more overshoot.
I (Integral)Eliminates steady-state error by accumulating past error. Adds a pole at origin → increases system type. Can cause overshoot and oscillation.
D (Derivative)Predicts future error trend. Adds damping, reduces overshoot. Sensitive to noise (high-frequency amplification).
Practical PID (with Derivative Filter) $$G_c(s) = K_p\!\left(1 + \frac{1}{T_i s} + \frac{T_d s}{1 + T_d s / N}\right)$$

The derivative term is filtered with $N$ (typically 8–20) to limit high-frequency gain.

◈ 8.2 — Ziegler-Nichols Tuning

Method 1: Step Response (Process Reaction Curve)

Apply a step to the open-loop plant. Measure delay $L$ and time constant $T$ from the S-shaped response:

Z-N Step Response Rules
$K_p$$T_i$$T_d$
P$T/L$∞0
PI$0.9\,T/L$$L/0.3$0
PID$1.2\,T/L$$2L$$0.5L$

Method 2: Ultimate Gain (Oscillation Method)

Increase $K_p$ (with $K_i=K_d=0$) until sustained oscillation. Record ultimate gain $K_u$ and period $T_u$:

Z-N Ultimate Gain Rules
$K_p$$T_i$$T_d$
P$0.5\,K_u$∞0
PI$0.45\,K_u$$T_u/1.2$0
PID$0.6\,K_u$$T_u/2$$T_u/8$

◈ 8.3 — Integrator Wind-Up

When the actuator saturates, the integral term continues accumulating error, causing large overshoot on recovery. Anti-windup strategies:

  • Clamping: Stop integrating when output is saturated
  • Back-calculation: Feed back the difference between saturated and unsaturated output to discharge the integrator
