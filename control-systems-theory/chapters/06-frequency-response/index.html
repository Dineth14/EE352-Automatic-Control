Frequency Response Methods

Bode & Nyquist Analysis

1. Bode Plots

The frequency response of a system $G(s)$ is found by evaluating $s = j\omega$. The Bode plot displays the log-magnitude and phase of $G(j\omega)$ against a logarithmic frequency axis.

$$ M(\omega)_{\text{dB}} = 20 \log_{10} |G(j\omega)| $$ $$ \phi(\omega) = \angle G(j\omega) $$ (6.1)

Interactive: Live Bode Plotter

Adjust system parameters (DC Gain, Zero, Pole pair) to see how the asymptotic Bode plot is constructed and how Phase Margin (PM) and Gain Margin (GM) change.

2. The Nyquist Criterion

The Nyquist plot is a polar plot of $G(j\omega)H(j\omega)$ as $\omega$ varies from $-\infty$ to $\infty$. Using Cauchy's Principle of Argument, stability is determined by counting encirclements of the $-1 + j0$ point.

Nyquist Stability Criterion:

$$ Z = N + P $$ (6.2)
  • $Z$: Number of closed-loop poles in the Right-Half Plane (must be 0 for stability).
  • $N$: Number of clockwise encirclements of the $-1+j0$ point.
  • $P$: Number of open-loop poles in the Right-Half Plane.

Observe the mapping between the $s$-plane contour (D-contour) and the $G(s)H(s)$-plane (Nyquist plot). Notice how encirclements of $-1$ determine stability.
